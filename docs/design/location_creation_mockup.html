Description
The Tidal Vaults sit below the harbour district, cut into the limestone bedrock during the city's first expansion. Salt water seeps through the walls at high tide and pools in the lower chambers. The smell hits you before the stairs do — brine and old iron and something else, something that has been left in the dark for too long. The locks are pre-empire. No one has changed them because no one agrees on who holds the keys.
History
Built as grain storage and a treasury annex under the third merchant council, the Vaults were seized during the harbour riots and have changed hands eleven times since. They have been used as a prison, a mint, a meeting place for at least two conspiracies, and briefly as a temple to a god whose name was later struck from the official records.
Dramatic role
The Vaults are a place where things go to be forgotten — documents, people, evidence. They embody the city's relationship with its own history: everyone knows something is down there, no one wants to be the one who opens it. Duke Orvassen has a chamber in the lower level. Caela Vorne's father died in the one next to it.
Signature line
